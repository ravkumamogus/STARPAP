Edo Japan had a belief in:
Technology

In 13th Century Edo Japan, the government set policies in place to trade wooden roofs for japanese kawara tiles to make it fire-resistant and stop fires from spreading.

Hongawara-buki tiles, a more artistic style of tiling that involved many different parts.

Sangawara-buki tiles, a more simplistic style of tiling that involved a single type of tile.

These tiles show how the government were able to improve society for their citizens. It is clear that Ieyasu didn't want his city burning down like what happened before him with Kanazawa castle, so he tried to prevent it by replacing their wood and straw roofs with clay tiles.
