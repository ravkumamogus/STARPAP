Art

This piece of art depicts a rebellion in Edo Japan against the Tokugawa Shogunate. In the Edo period, gold very popular in art. This piece of artwork is a woodblock print using ink and gold leaves. During the Edo Period, the government endorsed and promoted art that supported their beliefs and ideals, and as this piece showed how the government easily wiped out the rebellion, It was used as propaganda to discourage rebellions and get people to stay in line.

This art shows how the government used propaganda. Ieyasu wanted to keep his citizens loyal, so he promoted artists and art that fit his beliefs, creating propaganda without much effort for him.
